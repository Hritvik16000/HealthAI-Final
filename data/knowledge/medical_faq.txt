Q: What should I do after discharge if I have fever?
A: Contact your doctor or hospital immediately, especially if fever is persistent, high, or associated with worsening symptoms.

Q: What are common signs of dehydration?
A: Dry mouth, dizziness, fatigue, dark urine, and reduced urination are common signs.

Q: When should chest pain be treated as urgent?
A: Seek urgent medical care if chest pain is severe, persistent, or associated with breathlessness, sweating, nausea, or pain spreading to the arm or jaw.

Q: How can diabetes patients reduce infection risk?
A: Maintain glucose control, hydrate well, monitor wounds, and follow medication and hygiene guidance.

Q: What is the usual advice for long waiting time complaints?
A: Document the issue, report it to patient services, and seek immediate escalation if urgent care is delayed.

Q: What should a patient do if discharge instructions are unclear?
A: Ask the clinical team for clarification before leaving and request written instructions if needed.

Q: What are common symptoms of high blood pressure?
A: High blood pressure may be asymptomatic, but some patients report headache, dizziness, blurred vision, or chest discomfort.

Q: How should a smoker reduce cardiovascular risk?
A: Smoking cessation, blood pressure control, lipid management, exercise, and regular follow-up are common recommendations.
